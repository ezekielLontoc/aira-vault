# BOARD STATEMENT OF COMMITMENT

BFS Financial Services (BFS) is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the information assets entrusted to it by customers, employees, partners, regulators, and the broader Philippine financial system. The Board of Directors recognizes that information security is not solely a technical concern but a fundamental governance responsibility — inseparable from the prudent and ethical conduct of banking and financial services.

This Top-Level Information Security Policy expresses the Board's enduring commitment to establishing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS) that meets the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022, complies with all applicable Philippine and international regulations, and embodies the highest standards of trust expected of a regulated financial institution.

The Board commits the Organization to:

Treating information security as a strategic priority and a Board-level governance matter, with regular reporting to the Board Audit & Risk Committee and active oversight of the ISMS;

Providing the resources, funding, personnel, and authority necessary for the CIO, CISO, and supporting functions to operate the ISMS effectively;

Setting clear, measurable information security objectives aligned with BFS's business strategy, operational risk appetite, and regulatory obligations;

Ensuring all employees, contractors, and third parties acting on BFS's behalf understand and fulfill their information security responsibilities;

Maintaining a culture of accountability where information security failures are addressed, lessons learned are institutionalized, and continuous improvement is the operating norm;

Upholding customer trust through transparent, ethical, and lawful handling of personal and financial information.

This policy is approved by the Board of Directors and is binding on every member of the BFS organization, every contractor, and every third party operating in any capacity that touches BFS information assets. Compliance is mandatory; violations are subject to the disciplinary, contractual, and legal consequences described in this policy and its subordinate documents.

_______________________________

President, BFS Financial Services

# 1.  PURPOSE

This document is the Top-Level Information Security Policy of BFS Financial Services (hereinafter "the Organization" or "BFS"). It is the umbrella policy under which the entire BFS Information Security Management System (ISMS) operates. It expresses the Board's authoritative commitment to information security, establishes the governance and accountability framework for information security across the Organization, and defines the relationship to all subordinate policies, standards, procedures, and guidelines that implement the ISMS in operational detail.

This policy exists to fulfill the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 5.2, which mandates that top management establish an information security policy that is: appropriate to the purpose of the Organization, includes information security objectives or provides the framework for setting them, includes a commitment to satisfying applicable requirements, includes a commitment to continual improvement, is available as documented information, is communicated within the Organization, and is available to interested parties as appropriate. This policy meets each of these requirements explicitly.

This policy also exists to fulfill the parallel expectations of BSP Circular 1140 and BSP Circular 1048 for BSP-supervised institutions to maintain a Board-approved IT and cybersecurity risk management policy, and to align with international and Philippine financial sector best practices for governance of information security.

Beyond its compliance function, this policy serves as the foundational reference document for the entire BFS information security program. Every subordinate BFS-ISP technical control policy (BFS-ISP-001 through BFS-ISP-029) and every BFS-ISMS framework document (BFS-ISMS-002 through BFS-ISMS-010) derives its authority from, must remain consistent with, and is interpreted in light of this Top-Level Policy. When questions of authority, conflict between subordinate policies, or interpretation of intent arise, this Top-Level Policy is the controlling reference.

The specific objectives of this policy are to:

Express the Board's strategic commitment to information security as a fundamental governance responsibility of BFS, not merely a technical operational concern;

Establish the scope, principles, and governance structure of the BFS Information Security Management System;

Define the high-level information security objectives that the ISMS exists to achieve, and the framework for setting more detailed measurable objectives;

Allocate accountability for information security across the Board, executive leadership, management, employees, and third parties;

Provide the authoritative reference under which all subordinate BFS-ISP and BFS-ISMS policies operate;

Demonstrate to customers, regulators (BSP, NPC, SEC), business partners, auditors, certification bodies, and other interested parties that BFS treats information security with the seriousness expected of a regulated financial institution;

Establish the commitment to continual improvement of the ISMS as a permanent operating principle, not a one-time achievement.

# 2.  SCOPE OF THE INFORMATION SECURITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

The scope of the BFS ISMS — and therefore the scope of this Top-Level Policy and all subordinate BFS-ISP and BFS-ISMS documents — is defined by reference to the people, locations, processes, and information systems that BFS controls or that act on BFS's behalf in the conduct of its banking and financial services business. The full scope, with detailed boundaries, exclusions, and interfaces, is documented in BFS-ISMS-002 (ISMS Scope and Context Document); the present section provides the high-level scope statement.

## 2.1  Organizational Scope

This policy and the ISMS apply to:

All employees of BFS Financial Services, regardless of role, level, location, employment status (permanent, fixed-term, probationary), or business unit;

All contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and personnel from staffing agencies engaged by BFS in any capacity that involves access to BFS information, systems, or facilities;

All members of the Board of Directors and Board committees in their information-handling capacities related to BFS business;

All third-party service providers, vendors, outsourcing partners, and any other external entities processing BFS information or accessing BFS systems on BFS's behalf, governed contractually per BFS-ISP-015 (Service Provider Management);

All visitors, interviewees, customers, and other parties accessing BFS facilities, systems, or information in any controlled context.

## 2.2  Information Asset Scope

The ISMS protects all categories of information assets used in BFS operations:

Customer information: personal data (PII), sensitive personal information (SPI), account data, transaction history, payment card data (CHD), credit information, KYC documentation, and any other information relating to BFS customers (current, prospective, or former);

Employee and contractor information: personal data, employment records, payroll data, benefits information, performance records, and HR-related information;

Business information: financial records, strategic plans, board materials, legal documents, regulatory correspondence, intellectual property, internal policies, procedures, and operational data;

System and operational information: source code, system documentation, configuration data, audit logs, security telemetry, threat intelligence, and operational metrics;

Information from third parties: information received from regulators, counterparties, partners, vendors, and any other external source where BFS owes confidentiality or other handling obligations.

## 2.3  Technology and Process Scope

The ISMS covers all technology platforms, systems, applications, infrastructure, and processes that store, process, transmit, or protect BFS information:

All BFS-owned, BFS-leased, BFS-licensed, and BFS-operated information technology — including data centers, cloud environments (AWS), branch infrastructure, ATM networks, mobile and end-user devices, network infrastructure, applications, databases, and security systems;

All third-party hosted environments (SaaS, IaaS, PaaS) processing BFS information;

All physical premises housing BFS information assets — head office, branches, data centers, recovery sites, and any other BFS-controlled facilities;

All business processes that handle information — banking operations, payment processing, customer service, IT operations, risk management, compliance, internal audit, finance, HR, and corporate functions;

The development, acquisition, implementation, operation, and disposal of all systems and software within the BFS environment, governed by BFS-ISP-016 (Application Software Security) and other relevant policies.

## 2.4  Geographic Scope

This policy applies across all BFS locations and operations within the Republic of the Philippines, and to any cross-border activities, vendor arrangements, cloud regions, or business operations conducted on BFS's behalf outside the Philippines. Cross-border data transfers are subject to additional controls per RA 10173 and BSP regulations as documented in BFS-ISP-003 (Data Protection).

# 3.  POLICY STATEMENT AND INFORMATION SECURITY OBJECTIVES

## 3.1  Information Security Policy Statement

BFS Financial Services is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (the CIA Triad) of all information assets within the scope defined in Section 2, in proportion to their value, sensitivity, and the risks to which they are exposed. BFS shall:

Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an Information Security Management System aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and supporting frameworks (CIS Controls v8, NIST Cybersecurity Framework v2.0);

Comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual information security obligations — including BSP circulars, RA 10173, RA 10175, RA 11765, PCI DSS v4.0, and applicable international standards where BFS operations engage them;

Identify, assess, treat, and continuously monitor information security risks in alignment with the BFS enterprise risk appetite, using the methodology defined in BFS-ISMS-003 (Risk Assessment and Treatment Methodology);

Implement a documented and tested set of information security controls — both technical and organizational — sufficient to reduce identified risks to acceptable levels, as recorded in the BFS Statement of Applicability (BFS-ISMS-004);

Maintain the competence, awareness, and engagement of all personnel in fulfilling their information security responsibilities, governed by BFS-ISP-014 (Security Awareness and Training) and BFS-ISMS-007 (Competence, Awareness, and Communication Plan);

Detect, respond to, and learn from information security incidents in a manner that protects customers, BFS, and the broader financial system, governed by BFS-ISP-017 (Incident Response Management);

Ensure resilience and continuity of critical BFS services through tested business continuity and disaster recovery capabilities, governed by BFS-ISP-011 (Data Recovery) and the forthcoming BFS-ISP-011-BCP and BFS-ISP-011-DRP;

Continuously improve the ISMS through internal audit (BFS-ISMS-008), management review (BFS-ISMS-009), corrective action (BFS-ISMS-010), and lessons learned from incidents, exercises, and evolving threat intelligence.

# 4.  GUIDING PRINCIPLES

The following principles govern the design, implementation, and operation of the BFS Information Security Management System. These principles are non-negotiable design constraints that shape every subordinate policy, every architectural decision, and every operational choice within the ISMS.

## 4.1  Risk-Based Approach

Information security at BFS is risk-based, not checkbox-based. Controls are selected, designed, and operated in proportion to the risks they address — measured by likelihood, impact, and BFS's risk appetite. Resources are allocated to address the most consequential risks first. Compliance with frameworks (ISO 27001, CIS Controls, PCI DSS) is achieved as a consequence of effective risk management, not as a substitute for it.

## 4.2  Defense in Depth

BFS does not rely on any single control to protect any asset. Multiple, layered, independent controls are deployed so that the failure of any one control does not result in compromise. This principle is reflected throughout the BFS-ISP series — for example, BFS-ISP-010 (Malware Defenses) implements 10 layers of malware defense; BFS-ISP-013 (Network Monitoring and Defense) requires 12 mandatory sensor coverage points.

## 4.3  Least Privilege and Need-to-Know

Access to BFS information and systems is granted only to those who genuinely need it for their authorized duties, and only at the minimum level required. Privilege escalation is event-based, time-bounded, and audited. Standing administrative access is minimized. These principles are operationalized in BFS-ISP-005 (Account and Access Management) and BFS-ISP-006 (Access Control Management).

## 4.4  Zero Trust

BFS does not assume trust based on network location, device origin, or prior authentication. Every access request is authenticated, authorized, and validated against current policy regardless of where it originates. Internal network access is not equivalent to authorization. Zero Trust is BFS's strategic architectural direction, with implementation progressing through the BFS-ISP series and the MSAMS architecture blueprint.

## 4.5  Security and Privacy by Design

Information security and data privacy are designed into BFS systems from inception, not retrofitted after deployment. Every new system, application, integration, and significant change is reviewed for security and privacy implications before implementation begins. This principle governs BFS-ISP-016 (Application Software Security) SSDLC requirements and the forthcoming BFS-ISP-027 (Security in Project Management).

## 4.6  Auditability and Accountability

Every significant action affecting BFS information assets is logged, attributable to a specific actor, and reviewable by authorized parties. Audit trails are protected from tampering and retained per regulatory and legal requirements. This principle is operationalized in BFS-ISP-008 (Audit Log Management). Accountability is non-anonymous: shared accounts, generic identities, and untraceable actions are prohibited for any consequential operation.

## 4.7  Continuous Improvement

The ISMS is permanently in a Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle. Internal audits, management reviews, incident lessons learned, threat intelligence evolution, and regulatory updates continuously refine BFS controls. Improvement is not optional — it is the operating norm. This principle is operationalized through BFS-ISMS-008 (Internal Audit), BFS-ISMS-009 (Management Review), and BFS-ISMS-010 (Nonconformity and Corrective Action).

## 4.8  Proportionate Investment

BFS invests in information security in proportion to risk and business value, not based on the latest technology trends or vendor marketing. Investment decisions are evaluated against measurable risk reduction and contribution to strategic objectives. The ISMS shall not become an end in itself — it exists to protect BFS's ability to deliver banking and financial services to customers.

## 4.9  Transparency and Customer Trust

BFS is transparent with customers, regulators, and the public about its information security commitments and incidents. When incidents occur, BFS communicates honestly about scope, impact, and remediation. The Organization will not minimize, conceal, or delay disclosure of material security events. Customer trust depends not on the absence of incidents (which cannot be guaranteed) but on the honesty and competence with which incidents are addressed.

## 4.10  Ethical AI and Emerging Technology

BFS pioneers the use of artificial intelligence and emerging technologies in financial services with explicit ethical commitments: AI shall be used to serve customers and strengthen BFS's defenses, not to enable opaque or discriminatory decisioning, manipulate customers, or circumvent regulatory protections. AI governance is a first-class architectural concern, embedded throughout the AI-native core banking platform under construction.

# 5.  INFORMATION SECURITY GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

Effective information security requires clear allocation of authority, responsibility, and accountability across all levels of the Organization. The following governance structure operationalizes the Board's commitment to information security and ensures that the ISMS has the authority, resources, and oversight required to function effectively.

## 5.1  Three-Tier Governance Model

BFS information security governance operates on a three-tier model:

| Tier | Body / Function | Primary Responsibilities |

| --- | --- | --- |

| TIER 1 — Strategic | Board of Directors / Board Audit & Risk Committee | Ultimate accountability for information security. Approves the Top-Level Policy and major subordinate policies. Receives quarterly reporting on ISMS posture, material risks, and significant incidents. Reviews and approves the BFS Statement of Applicability and any material changes. Ensures adequate resources are committed to information security. |

| TIER 2 — Executive | CEO / CIO / CISO / CRO / DPO / CCO / Chief Legal Counsel | Operational accountability for ISMS implementation. Sets information security priorities aligned with business strategy. Approves operational policies and material risk acceptances. Coordinates information security across business units. Owns regulatory relationships (BSP, NPC, SEC) on information security matters. |

| TIER 3 — Operational | IT Security Team / Application Security / SOC / IT Operations / DevSecOps / Business Unit Information Security Coordinators | Day-to-day operation of information security controls. Implementation of subordinate BFS-ISP policies. Detection, response, and remediation of incidents. Operation of security tooling. Delivery of security services to business units. |

## 5.2  Board-Level Governance

The Board of Directors retains ultimate accountability for information security at BFS. Board-level information security governance is operationalized through:

The Board Audit & Risk Committee, which receives quarterly reporting on ISMS posture, material information security risks, significant incidents, regulatory compliance status, audit findings, and management review outputs;

Annual Board approval of this Top-Level Information Security Policy and any material amendments;

Board approval of the BFS Statement of Applicability (BFS-ISMS-004) and any material changes to applicable controls;

Board notification of CRITICAL (SEV-1) information security incidents at the time of occurrence and at incident closure (per BFS-ISP-017);

Board review of the annual Information Security Program Report covering posture, achievements, gaps, and forward-looking priorities;

Board ratification of material risk acceptances exceeding the CISO's delegated authority threshold.

## 5.3  Executive-Level Governance

Executive accountability for information security is allocated as follows:

| Executive Role | Information Security Accountability |

Represents BFS to the Board on material information security matters. Final approver for material customer notifications and external statements during CRITICAL incidents. |

| Chief Information Officer (CIO) | Accountable for the operational delivery of IT capabilities including information security. Co-owns this Top-Level Policy with the CISO. Ensures the IT organization has the structure, skills, and resources to operate the ISMS. Joint signatory on TIER 1 application engagements and material remediation decisions. Reports IT and security posture to the Board. |

Reports to the CIO with direct dotted-line access to the Board Audit & Risk Committee for matters affecting Board-level governance. |

| Chief Risk Officer (CRO) | Owns the enterprise risk management framework into which information security risk integrates. Reviews material information security risks for enterprise risk implications. Co-approves material risk acceptances. | Data Privacy Officer (DPO) | Statutorily required role under RA 10173. Owns personal data protection across BFS. Leads NPC notifications and data subject rights fulfillment. Co-owns BFS-ISP-003 (Data Protection) with the CISO. Independent reporting line to the Board on data privacy matters. |

## 5.4  Independence and Reporting Lines

To ensure independent oversight, the following reporting line principles apply:

The CISO maintains a direct dotted-line reporting relationship to the Board Audit & Risk Committee, enabling escalation of material information security matters independent of operational management;

The DPO has independent reporting authority to the Board on personal data protection matters, as required by RA 10173;

The Internal Audit function is independent of management — it reports administratively to the CEO but functionally to the Board Audit & Risk Committee, preserving its ability to assess the ISMS objectively;

The CRO and CCO maintain independent reporting authority to the Board on enterprise risk and compliance matters;

Information security incidents and material findings shall be reported through governance channels without filtering or modification by intermediate management, even when findings are unfavorable to that management.

## 5.5  Information Security Steering Committee

BFS shall maintain an Information Security Steering Committee chaired by the CISO and comprising representatives from IT, Risk, Compliance, Legal, Privacy, HR, Internal Audit, and major business units. The Steering Committee meets at least quarterly and:

Reviews ISMS posture, KPIs, incident trends, and audit findings;

Coordinates information security initiatives across business units;

Reviews and recommends approval of material policy changes before submission to executive or Board approval;

Reviews material risk acceptances and provides input on cross-functional risk decisions;

Champions information security culture and awareness initiatives across BFS.

# 9.  CONTINUAL IMPROVEMENT OF THE ISMS

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 5.2 requires a commitment to continual improvement of the ISMS. BFS commits to continual improvement not as a compliance formality but as the operating philosophy of the security program. The ISMS is permanently in motion — refining controls, learning from incidents, adapting to threats, and raising the bar.

## 9.1  The Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) Cycle

The BFS ISMS operates on the Plan-Do-Check-Act improvement cycle:

| Phase | Activities | BFS Implementation |

| --- | --- | --- |

| PLAN | Establish ISMS context, scope, policy, objectives, risk assessment, treatment plan | Defined in BFS-ISMS-001 (this Top-Level Policy), BFS-ISMS-002 (Scope), BFS-ISMS-003 (Risk Methodology), BFS-ISMS-004 (SoA), BFS-ISMS-005 (Objectives). Reviewed and updated annually. |

| DO | Implement and operate the ISMS — controls, procedures, training, monitoring | Operationalized through the BFS-ISP series of technical control policies, supported by BFS-ISMS-006 (Document Control), BFS-ISMS-007 (Competence and Awareness). |

| CHECK | Monitor, measure, analyze, and evaluate ISMS performance — internal audits, monitoring, KPIs, management review | Performed through KPI tracking in each BFS-ISP policy, internal audits per BFS-ISMS-008, penetration testing per BFS-ISP-018, and management review per BFS-ISMS-009. |

| ACT | Take actions to continually improve the ISMS — corrective actions, preventive actions, improvements | Implemented through BFS-ISMS-010 (Nonconformity and Corrective Action), incident lessons learned per BFS-ISP-017, audit finding remediation, and continuous policy improvement. |

## 9.2  Sources of Improvement

The ISMS draws improvement input from multiple continuous sources:

Internal audit findings: identifying control gaps, policy deviations, and effectiveness issues per BFS-ISMS-008;

Management review outputs: strategic direction, resource decisions, scope changes per BFS-ISMS-009;

Incident lessons learned: root cause analysis from every CRITICAL/HIGH incident per BFS-ISP-017;

Penetration testing findings: adversarial validation of control effectiveness per BFS-ISP-018;

Vulnerability management trends: emerging weaknesses and remediation patterns per BFS-ISP-007;

Threat intelligence: evolving attacker tradecraft, novel attack patterns, sector-specific threats;

Regulatory updates: new BSP circulars, NPC issuances, PCI DSS revisions, international guidance;

Tabletop exercises and Purple Team engagements: process and capability validation per BFS-ISP-017 and BFS-ISP-018;

Customer, employee, and partner feedback: signals about user experience, training effectiveness, control friction;

Industry best practice evolution: financial sector ISACs (FS-ISAC, BSP-ISAC), peer benchmarking, emerging frameworks.

## 9.3  Improvement Tracking

All identified improvements are tracked through the BFS Improvement Register maintained by the CISO Office. Each improvement item has: identified source, owner, target completion date, status, and validation criteria. Improvement items are reported quarterly to the Information Security Steering Committee and annually to the Board Audit & Risk Committee. Improvements that exceed 18 months without completion are escalated for executive review and resource decision.

## 9.4  Continuous Improvement is a Cultural Commitment

Beyond formal procedures, BFS commits to a culture in which continuous improvement is everyone's responsibility. Employees are encouraged to identify and report opportunities for improving information security practices through normal channels. Improvement suggestions are evaluated on merit, not source. The Organization will not punish or marginalize employees who identify uncomfortable findings — to the contrary, surfacing improvement opportunities is recognized and rewarded.

# 11.  ENFORCEMENT

Compliance with this Top-Level Policy and all subordinate ISMS documents is mandatory for every member of the BFS organization, every contractor, and every third party operating in any capacity that touches BFS information assets. Enforcement is proportionate to the nature, severity, and impact of the violation.

## 11.1  Disciplinary Framework

Violations are addressed through the following framework, with enforcement action determined by the relevant authority (HR for employees, contracting authority for contractors and third parties, regulatory and law enforcement authorities for criminal matters):

| Severity | Examples | Consequences |

| --- | --- | --- |

| Minor | Inadvertent procedural deviation; late completion of mandatory training; minor failure to follow documented procedure without harm. | Coaching; mandatory remediation; supervisory follow-up; documentation in performance review. |

| Moderate | Repeated minor violations; failure to report a suspected security event through proper channels; using BFS systems for unauthorized personal purposes that consume material resources. | Formal warning; mandatory retraining; access review; documented in performance review; potential impact on performance evaluation. |

| Serious | Sharing credentials; bypassing security controls without authorization; deploying production changes without security gate approval; failing to comply with regulatory notification obligations within required timeframes. | Suspension pending investigation; formal HR disciplinary process up to termination; immediate remediation; CISO and HR escalation; potential financial consequences for BFS impact. |

| Critical / Legal | Deliberate unauthorized access to systems or data; exfiltration of customer data; deliberate concealment of security incidents; theft, fraud, or other criminal acts using BFS systems; coordination with threat actors; deliberate sabotage of BFS information assets. | Immediate termination; referral to NBI Cybercrime Division or PNP-ACG for criminal prosecution under RA 10175; civil action for damages; mandatory regulatory notifications (NPC, BSP); permanent disqualification from working with BFS in any capacity. |

## 11.2  No Retaliation for Good-Faith Reporting

BFS commits to a non-retaliation policy: employees, contractors, or third parties reporting suspected security violations, raising concerns about ISMS effectiveness, or surfacing uncomfortable findings in good faith shall not be disadvantaged in employment, evaluation, contracting, or business relationship as a consequence of that reporting. Retaliation against good-faith reporters is itself a serious policy violation subject to disciplinary action up to termination.

## 11.3  Whistleblower Protection

BFS maintains a confidential whistleblower channel for reporting suspected violations of law, regulation, or BFS policy — including information security violations. Reports may be made anonymously and are investigated by Internal Audit and Legal independent of operational management. Whistleblower protection extends to information security matters.

# 12.  EXCEPTIONS HANDLING

## 12.1  Exception Principles at the Top-Level

Exceptions to this Top-Level Policy are extraordinary and require Board-level approval. The Top-Level Policy expresses fundamental commitments — to risk management, defense in depth, regulatory compliance, customer trust — that do not lend themselves to operational exceptions. Exceptions to subordinate BFS-ISP and BFS-ISMS policies are governed by their respective exception sections, with appropriate executive approval authority.

## 12.2  No Exceptions Permissible

The following commitments in this Top-Level Policy admit no exception under any circumstances:

Compliance with applicable law and regulation (BSP circulars, RA 10173, RA 10175, etc.) — these are legal obligations that cannot be waived by internal policy exception;

Board-level oversight of the ISMS — this is an ISO 27001 Clause 5.1 requirement and a fundamental governance commitment;

Customer notification of personal data breaches as required by RA 10173 and NPC Circular 16-03 — within the 72-hour clock and subject to NPC's specific content requirements;

Independence of the Internal Audit, DPO, and CISO reporting lines — these protect the integrity of governance and cannot be waived;

Non-retaliation for good-faith reporting — the integrity of the whistleblower and reporting channels is essential to ISMS effectiveness.

## 12.3  Subordinate Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to subordinate BFS-ISP and BFS-ISMS policies are governed by the exception provisions of those individual policies, with approval authority commensurate with the scope and impact of the exception. The BFS-ISP series uniformly requires that exceptions be: time-bounded, accompanied by compensating controls, formally approved at the appropriate authority level, logged in the relevant exception register, reviewed periodically for continued justification, and treated as policy violations if they expire without renewal or remediation.

## 12.4  Exception Process for This Top-Level Policy

If an exception to this Top-Level Policy is sought (an extraordinary circumstance), a written exception request shall be submitted to the CISO with full justification, risk assessment, compensating controls, duration, and remediation plan.

The CISO reviews the request with the CIO, CRO, DPO, CCO, and Chief Legal Counsel. If the request relates to a non-waivable commitment (Section 12.2), the request is refused at this stage.

If executive review concludes the exception is appropriate, it is presented to the Board Audit & Risk Committee for review and recommendation.

Board approval is required for any approved Top-Level Policy exception. Approved exceptions are recorded in the Board minutes and reviewed at every subsequent Board meeting until expiration or remediation.

Top-Level Policy exceptions are exceptional events — the absence of any such exception in a calendar year is the expected state.
