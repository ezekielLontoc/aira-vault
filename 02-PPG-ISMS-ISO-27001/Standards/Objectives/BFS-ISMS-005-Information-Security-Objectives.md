# 1.  PURPOSE

This document defines the BFS Financial Services Information Security Objectives for the calendar year 2026, fulfilling the mandate of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.2. It translates the eight strategic information security objectives established at Board level in BFS-ISMS-001 §3.2 into specific, measurable, time-bound, accountable operational objectives that drive the BFS information security program throughout the year.

The BFS-ISMS-001 strategic objectives express what BFS commits to achieve at the highest level — protecting customer trust, maintaining regulatory compliance, enabling secure innovation, building operational resilience, developing a security-conscious culture, governing third-party and supply chain risk, embedding AI governance, and continuously improving. This document operationalizes those commitments into the specific targets, measurements, and accountable parties that determine whether the strategic objectives are actually being achieved.

Without this operationalization, strategic objectives remain aspirational language. With this operationalization, the BFS Board, executive leadership, regulators, and certification bodies can examine concrete evidence of progress against measurable targets. ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.2 specifically requires that information security objectives be measurable (where practicable) and that BFS determine what will be done, what resources will be required, who will be responsible, when actions will be completed, and how results will be evaluated. This document provides each of these elements explicitly.

This document is the primary input to the quarterly Board Audit & Risk Committee reporting on information security posture, the annual ISMS Management Review per BFS-ISMS-009, and external reporting to regulators and certification bodies. Quarterly progress against each operational objective is the core mechanism by which BFS leadership validates that the information security program is on track — or identifies course corrections required.

The specific objectives of this document are to:

Translate the eight BFS-ISMS-001 strategic objectives into specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) operational objectives for calendar year 2026;

Establish the measurement methodologies, data sources, and reporting cadences for each operational objective;

Allocate accountability for each operational objective to a specific named role or function — ensuring no objective is unowned;

Document the resource requirements, dependencies, and key risks for achievement of each objective;

Provide the quarterly tracking framework that the Board Audit & Risk Committee uses to assess progress;

Support annual ISMS Management Review (per BFS-ISMS-009) with prior-year achievement evidence and current-year progress;

Provide certification audit evidence demonstrating compliance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.2.

# 3.  OBJECTIVES FRAMEWORK

## 3.1  Three-Tier Objectives Hierarchy

BFS information security objectives operate on a three-tier hierarchy that ensures alignment from Board strategy through to operational execution. This document is the middle tier — translating Board strategy into operational targets that subsequently drive operational KPIs in subordinate policies.

| Tier | Document | Time Horizon | Audience |

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| TIER 1 — Strategic Objectives | BFS-ISMS-001 §3.2 | Multi-year (3-5 years) — foundational commitments | Board of Directors; external interested parties (regulators, customers, certification bodies) |

| TIER 2 — Operational Objectives | BFS-ISMS-005 (this document) | Annual — calendar year targets | Board Audit & Risk Committee (quarterly); Executive leadership; Information Security Steering Committee |

| TIER 3 — Operational KPIs | Each BFS-ISP §10 (Compliance and Enforcement KPIs) | Continuous — operational measurement | CISO Office; operational teams; quarterly reporting |

Each TIER 2 operational objective in Section 4 maps to a TIER 1 strategic objective above and to one or more TIER 3 operational KPIs below. This bidirectional traceability ensures that strategic commitments are operationalized into measurable execution and that operational performance rolls up into strategic progress reporting.

## 3.2  SMART Criteria Application

Each operational objective in this document satisfies the SMART criteria — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. The application of SMART at BFS is:

| SMART Criterion | BFS Application |

| --- | --- |

| Specific | Each objective addresses a specific information security outcome with sufficient precision that all parties understand what success looks like. Generic objectives ('improve security') are not acceptable. |

| Measurable | Each objective has a quantitative target where practicable (e.g., '≤ 5% phishing simulation click rate', '100% completion of mandatory training'). Where quantitative measurement is impractical, qualitative measurement criteria with documented evaluation method are specified. |

| Achievable | Targets are set at levels that are challenging but achievable given BFS resources, current capability maturity, and timeline. Aspirational targets that everyone knows are unachievable degrade the credibility of the program. |

| Relevant | Each objective traces to a BFS-ISMS-001 strategic objective and addresses material risk, regulatory obligation, or interested party requirement. Objectives that do not advance BFS information security strategy are not included. |

| Time-bound | Each objective specifies the period over which it applies (typically calendar year 2026) and any internal milestones (quarterly progress thresholds, completion deadlines). |

## 5.2  Key Dependencies

Achievement of CY2026 objectives depends on the following key dependencies. Material changes to these dependencies trigger objective adjustment per Section 7.

Adequate funding per the Board-approved CY2026 information security budget;

Recruitment success for key roles — particularly application security, AI security, and SOC analyst positions in a tight talent market;

Continuity of strategic technology investments including SIEM/EDR upgrades, PAM consolidation, and AI governance tooling;

Successful execution of the AI-native core banking platform development per the architectural roadmap;

Cooperation across business units in implementing controls within their respective domains;

Stable regulatory environment — material new BSP circulars, NPC issuances, or PCI DSS updates may shift priorities;

Continued availability of qualified external service providers (testing firms, IR retainers, MSSPs) per existing arrangements;

Service Provider security posture stability — material vendor security incidents would shift focus to investigation and supply chain risk response.

| Status | Definition | Threshold Examples | Action |

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| GREEN | On track to achieve objective by target date | Performance ≥ 90% of target by milestone; trend favorable | Continue monitoring; no special action required |

| AMBER | Concerns about achievement; corrective action needed | Performance 70-89% of target; trend uncertain; resource issues identified | Documented corrective action plan; escalation to executive leadership; resource adjustment consideration |

| RED | Material risk of not achieving objective; intervention required | Performance < 70% of target; trend unfavorable; achievement unlikely without significant intervention | Immediate Board notification; resource reallocation; objective revision may be required; Section 7 update process |

## 6.3  Communication of Objectives Per Clause 6.2(e)

Per ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.2(e), objectives must be communicated to relevant interested parties. BFS communication of these objectives:

Internal: Quarterly Board Audit & Risk Committee reports; quarterly Information Security Steering Committee briefings; monthly executive leadership summaries; quarterly all-staff communications about high-level progress (with appropriate confidentiality);

External — Regulators: BSP cyber maturity assessment evidence; PCI QSA assessment evidence; NPC compliance reporting; SEC disclosure where applicable;

External — Certification Body: Annual certification audit and surveillance audit evidence; objectives progress as ISMS effectiveness demonstration;

External — Customers and Partners: High-level commitments expressed in BFS-ISMS-001 (PUBLIC); specific measurable progress not generally externalized to protect operational confidentiality;

Internal — All Staff: Awareness program reinforcement of relevant objectives (especially O5 culture objectives) per BFS-ISP-014.

# 7.  REVIEW AND UPDATES

## 7.1  Quarterly Review Process

Each calendar quarter, the CISO Office executes the following objectives review process:

Collect performance data for all 41 operational objectives from underlying KPI sources, manual measurements, and external evidence;

Calculate quarterly progress against annual targets; assign RAG status per Section 6.2 convention;

Identify objectives at AMBER or RED status; develop documented corrective action plans;

Prepare quarterly Board Audit & Risk Committee report with executive summary, dashboard view, and detailed analysis;

Present to Information Security Steering Committee for discussion and corrective action endorsement;

Present to Board Audit & Risk Committee at scheduled meeting;

Document Board feedback and any direction changes;

Communicate updated focus areas to operational teams.

## 7.2  Mid-Year Recalibration

Following Q2 review (mid-year), a structured recalibration is performed to assess whether annual objectives remain achievable, appropriate, and aligned with current conditions:

Objectives where Q2 progress is materially behind target are evaluated for: (a) remediation strategy; (b) target adjustment; (c) resource reallocation;

Objectives where conditions have changed materially (regulatory shifts, threat landscape, business changes) are reviewed for continued relevance;

New objectives may be proposed if material gaps emerge during the year;

Material adjustments approved by Board Audit & Risk Committee.

## 7.3  Annual Review and Reset

Annually, in Q4, BFS conducts a comprehensive review and reset of objectives for the following calendar year:

Year-end performance assessment for all CY2026 objectives — achieved / partially achieved / not achieved with rationale;

Lessons learned from objective definition, measurement, and pursuit;

Updated risk environment per the latest BFS-ISMS-003 Risk Register state;

Updated Statement of Applicability (BFS-ISMS-004) implementation status;

Updated regulatory landscape and interested party expectations;

Updated strategic objectives if BFS-ISMS-001 §3.2 has been amended;

Drafting of CY2026 objectives following the same SMART framework;

Approval of CY2026 objectives by the Board Audit & Risk Committee in Q4;

CY2026 objectives effective January of next year; this document superseded by the new annual issuance.

## 7.4  Out-of-Cycle Updates

Material events between scheduled reviews may require out-of-cycle objective updates:

Material changes to BFS strategy or business model affecting objective relevance;

New regulatory obligations creating new compliance objectives;

Significant CRITICAL security incident revealing systemic gaps requiring new objectives;

Material changes to threat landscape demanding response;

Material changes to BFS-ISMS-001 strategic objectives requiring downstream operational alignment.

Out-of-cycle updates require the same approval process as the annual document — CISO + CIO + CRO recommendation; Information Security Steering Committee endorsement; Board Audit & Risk Committee approval.
