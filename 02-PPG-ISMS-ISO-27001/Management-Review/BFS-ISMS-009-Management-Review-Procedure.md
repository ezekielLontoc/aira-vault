# 1.  PURPOSE

This document establishes the BFS Financial Services procedure for Management Review of the Information Security Management System (ISMS), fulfilling the mandate of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3. Management Review is the formal forum at which top management reviews the ISMS at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness — and makes strategic decisions about ISMS direction, resource allocation, and change.

Management Review is the strategic counterpart to operational Internal Audit (BFS-ISMS-008). Where Internal Audit provides independent operational assurance — verifying that documented controls are operating as designed — Management Review provides strategic synthesis at top management level. The CISO and operational ISMS functions cannot, by themselves, determine whether the ISMS continues to serve BFS strategic objectives in a changing risk and regulatory environment. That determination requires top management deliberation drawing on inputs from the entire ISMS — and that is what Management Review provides.

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3 has three sub-clauses with explicit requirements:

Clause 9.3.1 (General) — top management shall review the organization's ISMS at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness;

Clause 9.3.2 (Management Review Inputs) — specifies seven mandatory inputs that the management review shall consider: (a) status of actions from previous management reviews; (b) changes in external and internal issues relevant to the ISMS; (c) changes in needs and expectations of interested parties; (d) feedback on information security performance including trends in nonconformities, monitoring results, audit results, and fulfillment of information security objectives; (e) feedback from interested parties; (f) results of risk assessment and status of risk treatment plan; (g) opportunities for continual improvement;

Clause 9.3.3 (Management Review Results) — outputs of management review shall include decisions related to continual improvement opportunities and any need for changes to the ISMS. Documented information shall be retained as evidence of the results of management reviews.

Clause 9.3 is deceptively short but consequential. Many organizations conduct 'management reviews' that are presentations to executives without genuine deliberation, decision-making, or documented outputs. ISO 27001 certification audits routinely identify Clause 9.3 weaknesses in such organizations — finding that the seven mandatory inputs were not all addressed, that decisions were not documented, that prior review actions were not tracked to closure, or that the review forum was actually the Information Security Steering Committee rather than top management. This procedure prevents these weaknesses by establishing explicit structure: bi-annual cadence at the Board Audit & Risk Committee level, comprehensive input package addressing all seven Clause 9.3.2 requirements, structured deliberation and decision-making, documented minutes recording decisions, and formal action tracking through to closure.

Beyond ISO 27001 compliance, this procedure fulfills BSP supervisory expectations for Board oversight of IT risk and cybersecurity (BSP Circulars 1140 and 1048), SEC Code of Corporate Governance expectations for Board oversight of material risks, RA 8791 Board fiduciary duties in supervised institutions, and the broader expectations of regulators, certification bodies, and other interested parties for genuine top management engagement with information security.

The specific objectives of this document are to:

Establish the bi-annual Management Review forum at the Board Audit & Risk Committee level — the appropriate top management body for a regulated financial institution;

Specify the comprehensive input package ensuring all seven Clause 9.3.2 mandatory inputs are systematically addressed at every Management Review;

Define the Management Review process — preparation, conduct, decision-making, minute production, action tracking;

Mandate the output decisions required per Clause 9.3.3 — continual improvement opportunities and changes to the ISMS;

Establish the linkages between Management Review outputs and the operational ISMS — how decisions translate into BFS-ISMS-005 objective updates, BFS-ISMS-003 risk register changes, BFS-ISMS-010 corrective action assignments, and resource allocation;

Document records retention requirements supporting ISO 27001 certification audit and regulatory examination evidence needs;

Provide audit-friendly documentation of Clause 9.3 compliance — auditors examining Management Review can trace from this procedure to conducted reviews to documented decisions.

# 2.  DEFINITIONS AND SCOPE

## 2.1  Definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following definitions apply:

| Term | Definition |

| --- | --- |

| Management Review | Formal review of the ISMS by top management at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness — making decisions related to continual improvement and changes to the ISMS. (ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3) |

| Top Management | Person or group of people who directs and controls an organization at the highest level. At BFS, top management for ISMS purposes is the Board Audit & Risk Committee (functioning as Board delegate) supported by the CEO and the executive team. (ISO/IEC 27000:2018) |

| Continuing Suitability | The extent to which the ISMS remains aligned with BFS strategic context, scope, and direction over time. Suitability deteriorates when scope, strategy, regulatory environment, or threat landscape evolve without corresponding ISMS evolution. |

| Adequacy | The extent to which the ISMS, as designed, is sufficient to meet BFS information security objectives and regulatory requirements. Adequacy is a question of design completeness — does the ISMS cover what it needs to cover. |

| Effectiveness | The extent to which the ISMS, as operated, achieves intended results. Effectiveness is a question of operational execution — does the ISMS actually produce the outcomes it was designed to produce. (ISO/IEC 27000:2018) |

| Management Review Forum | The formal meeting at which Management Review is conducted. At BFS, the bi-annual Management Review is conducted at scheduled Board Audit & Risk Committee meetings with extended agenda dedicated to ISMS review. |

| Management Review Input Package | The comprehensive documentation prepared by the CISO Office addressing all seven Clause 9.3.2 mandatory inputs and supplementary materials. Distributed to Forum participants at minimum 14 days before the Forum date. |

| Management Review Minutes | The documented record of the Management Review — including attendees, materials reviewed, deliberations summary, decisions made, actions assigned, target dates, and accountabilities. Constitute the documented information required by Clause 9.3.3. |

| Management Review Action | Specific commitment recorded in Management Review Minutes including: action description, accountable party (named role), target completion date, and tracking mechanism. Closed when action is verified completed. |

| Continual Improvement Opportunity | Identified potential ISMS enhancement that exceeds correction of nonconformity. Per ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 10.1 — improvement opportunities are deliberate enhancements rather than fixes for known problems. |

| ISMS Change | Material modification to the ISMS — scope, methodology, controls, objectives, organizational structure — typically requiring revision of TIER 1 or TIER 2 documents and implementation planning. |

| Interested Party | Person or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by an ISMS decision or activity. (ISO/IEC 27000:2018) BFS interested parties documented in BFS-ISMS-002 §3. |

| Performance Trend | Pattern of change over time in measurable ISMS performance indicators. Trends are particularly important inputs to Management Review — single-period snapshots are less informative than directional trends. |

| External Issue | Environmental factor outside BFS that affects the ISMS — regulatory changes, threat landscape evolution, sector developments, technology evolution, geopolitical events. (Per ISO/IEC 27000:2018 and BFS-ISMS-002) |

| Internal Issue | Organizational factor within BFS that affects the ISMS — strategic direction changes, organizational restructuring, capability evolution, resource changes, technology architecture changes. (Per ISO/IEC 27000:2018 and BFS-ISMS-002) |

## 2.2  Scope

This procedure applies to the Management Review of the BFS ISMS as defined in BFS-ISMS-002 (Scope and Context). Specifically:

### 2.2.1  In Scope

Bi-annual scheduled Management Reviews conducted at the Board Audit & Risk Committee — the principal mechanism for fulfilling Clause 9.3;

Out-of-cycle Management Reviews triggered by material events — significant security incidents, material regulatory changes, scope changes, or specific Board direction;

All preparation, conduct, output, and follow-up activities associated with Management Review;

Management Review of all ISMS components within scope per BFS-ISMS-002 — governance, risk management, controls, objectives, audit findings, corrective actions, performance metrics.

### 2.2.2  Out of Scope

Operational management forums (Information Security Steering Committee meetings, CISO operational meetings) — these are operational governance, not Management Review per Clause 9.3. Operational forums feed into Management Review preparation but do not substitute for it;

Board strategic planning sessions covering broader BFS strategy — though information security may be discussed, these are not ISMS Management Reviews per Clause 9.3 unless explicitly designated;

Functional reviews within specific business units or IT functions — these are operational activities, not ISMS-level Management Review;

Quarterly Board reporting on information security posture per BFS-ISMS-005 — this is Board awareness and oversight, distinct from the formal Management Review forum which is more comprehensive in inputs and deliberative in outputs.

### 2.2.3  Distinction Between Forums

BFS distinguishes between several governance forums to prevent conflation. Each has distinct purpose, frequency, participants, and outputs:

| Forum | Frequency | Primary Participants | Distinguishing Purpose |

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| ISMS Management Review (this document) | Bi-annual | Board Audit & Risk Committee + CEO + senior executives | Strategic ISMS review per Clause 9.3 with formal decisions |

| Board Audit & Risk Committee — Quarterly | Quarterly | Committee members + CISO + Internal Audit + CRO | Quarterly oversight of audit findings, risks, and ISMS posture |

| Information Security Steering Committee | Quarterly | Cross-functional executives + CISO | Operational ISMS governance and cross-functional coordination |

| Executive Leadership Brief | Monthly | CEO + C-suite | Executive operational awareness; not Management Review |

| CISO Operational Meeting | Weekly | CISO Office + key operational leads | Operational execution; not Management Review |

# 3.  MANAGEMENT REVIEW FRAMEWORK

This section establishes the structural framework for BFS Management Review — the cadence, forum designation, participants, and authority — within which specific reviews are conducted per the procedure in Section 5.

## 3.1  Management Review Cadence

BFS conducts ISMS Management Review bi-annually — twice per calendar year — at scheduled Board Audit & Risk Committee meetings:

| Review | Target Month | Primary Focus | Output Timeline |

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Mid-Year Management Review | Q2 (May or June) | Mid-year status assessment; H1 audit and incident summary; H1 progress against annual objectives; corrective action of any emerging issues | Decisions effective Q3 |

| Annual Management Review | Q4 (November or early December) | Full-year retrospective; comprehensive evidence package; CY+1 objectives approval; annual ISMS effectiveness opinion; strategic direction-setting | Decisions effective January (next CY) |

| Out-of-Cycle Reviews | As triggered | Material events: significant incidents (CRITICAL with material customer/regulatory impact); material regulatory changes; material scope changes; specific Board direction | Decisions effective per urgency |

The bi-annual cadence is calibrated to BFS scale, regulatory expectations, and the rate of change in the information security environment. More frequent Management Reviews are administratively burdensome without adding strategic value; less frequent reviews allow strategic drift between reviews. Bi-annual cadence balances both considerations and exceeds the ISO 27001 minimum (Clause 9.3 requires only 'planned intervals' without specifying frequency).

| CADENCE RATIONALE:  ISO 27001 does not mandate bi-annual reviews — it requires 'planned intervals'. Many organizations conduct annual Management Reviews and pass certification. BFS chooses bi-annual cadence because: (a) the BFS information security environment changes rapidly, particularly with AI-native platform development; (b) BSP supervisory expectations for Board oversight in regulated institutions favor more frequent engagement; (c) bi-annual cadence allows mid-year course-correction rather than waiting until annual reset; (d) the Board Audit & Risk Committee already meets quarterly, so Management Review extension is incremental burden; (e) bi-annual reviews provide stronger ISO 27001 certification audit evidence than annual minimum. |

## 3.2  Management Review Forum

BFS designates the Board Audit & Risk Committee — supplemented by the CEO and senior executives in attendance — as the Management Review Forum. This designation reflects:

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3 requirement that 'top management' conduct the review — at BFS, the Board Audit & Risk Committee is the appropriate top management body for ISMS oversight, with delegated authority from the full Board;

BSP supervisory expectations that Board-level forums oversee material risks including IT and cybersecurity (per BSP Circulars 1140 and 1048);

Independence of audit and risk oversight from operational management — ensuring that Management Review is genuinely top management deliberation rather than executive presentation;

Established meeting cadence and governance protocols of the Committee — leveraging existing infrastructure rather than creating duplicative forums;

Direct linkage to the Internal Audit function (BFS-ISMS-008) which reports functionally to the Committee — ensuring audit findings flow directly into Management Review without intermediation;

Authority to make binding decisions affecting BFS strategy, resource allocation, and policy — Management Review is consequential, not advisory.

## 3.3  Forum Participants

Management Review participants are categorized as Decision-Making Participants (with deliberation and decision authority), Standing Attendees (with information and advisory roles), and Specific Attendees (invited as agenda warrants):

| Participant | Category | Role at Management Review |

| --- | --- | --- |

| Board Audit & Risk Committee Members | Decision-Making | Deliberate on inputs; make decisions per Clause 9.3.3; provide strategic direction; approve material ISMS changes; approve forthcoming-year information security objectives |

| Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair | Decision-Making | Chairs the Management Review session; ensures all Clause 9.3.2 inputs are addressed; manages deliberation; signs off Minutes |

| Chief Executive Officer | Decision-Making | Represents executive management commitment; ensures decisions translate to executive action; final authority on resource allocation between ISMS and other priorities |

| Chief Information Officer | Decision-Making | IT leadership perspective; accountability for IT-domain decisions; champions IT enablement of ISMS |

| Chief Information Security Officer | Standing Attendee | Presents the Management Review Input Package; provides expert response to Committee questions; accountable for executing operational implications of decisions |

| Chief Risk Officer | Standing Attendee | Enterprise risk perspective; integrates ISMS findings with broader risk picture; supports risk-related decisions |

| Chief Compliance Officer | Standing Attendee | Regulatory perspective; identifies compliance implications of decisions; coordinates regulatory engagement |

| Data Privacy Officer | Standing Attendee | RA 10173 and privacy perspective; reports on personal data protection; independent reporting line preserves independence |

| Head of Internal Audit | Standing Attendee | Independent assurance perspective; presents audit programme outputs; provides independent ISMS effectiveness opinion |

| Chief Legal Counsel | Specific Attendee | Invited when legal or regulatory matters are on agenda — material litigation, enforcement actions, contract issues |

| Chief Human Resources Officer | Specific Attendee | Invited when competence, awareness, or HR-related matters are material to agenda |

| Chief Technology Officer | Specific Attendee | Invited when technology architecture, AI platform, or significant IT change matters are material to agenda |

| External Specialists | Specific Attendee | Invited as appropriate — ISO 27001 certification body representative, external auditors, regulatory liaison, advisory firm representatives |

## 3.4  Quorum and Decision-Making

Management Review quorum and decision-making protocols ensure that decisions reflect genuine top management deliberation:

Quorum: Minimum participation by Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair, two additional Committee members, CEO (or designated executive substitute with prior approval), and CISO. Without quorum, the Management Review cannot validly produce decisions and is rescheduled;

Deliberation: Decisions emerge from Committee deliberation following input package review and presentation. Sufficient time is allocated for genuine deliberation — typical Management Review takes 90-180 minutes;

Decision Authority: The Board Audit & Risk Committee, by Board delegation, has authority to make ISMS decisions per Clause 9.3.3. Decisions affecting BFS strategy, material resource allocation, or fundamental ISMS structure are escalated to full Board for approval;

Consensus Preference: Committee decisions preferentially reach consensus. Where consensus is not reached, decisions are made by majority of Decision-Making Participants present, with dissenting positions documented in Minutes;

Documentation: All decisions are documented in Minutes per Section 5.4 — undocumented decisions cannot be relied upon for ISO 27001 evidence regardless of actual deliberation.

## 3.5  Out-of-Cycle Management Review Triggers

In addition to scheduled bi-annual reviews, the following events trigger out-of-cycle Management Review:

CRITICAL security incident with material customer impact, material regulatory exposure, or potential systemic implications — out-of-cycle review held within 30 days of incident closure;

Material adverse finding from BSP examination, NPC compliance review, or ISO 27001 certification audit requiring strategic response;

Material change to BFS scope (e.g., acquisition, divestiture, new business line) requiring ISMS scope adjustment;

Material change to regulatory environment (e.g., new BSP circular significantly affecting ISMS expectations, RA 10173 amendments, PCI DSS major version change);

Specific direction from the Board (full Board, not just the Committee) to conduct out-of-cycle Management Review;

Identification of fundamental ISMS effectiveness concern by Internal Audit, the CISO, the CRO, or other senior leadership.

Out-of-cycle Management Reviews follow the same procedure as scheduled reviews per Section 5, scoped to the triggering event and supported by an abbreviated input package focused on the relevant concerns. Out-of-cycle reviews supplement rather than replace the next scheduled review.

# 4.  REQUIRED MANAGEMENT REVIEW INPUTS

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3.2 specifies seven mandatory inputs that every Management Review shall consider. This section defines each input, identifies the BFS-internal data sources, and specifies the materials prepared for each Management Review. Every BFS Management Review systematically addresses all seven inputs — not selectively, not as time permits, but comprehensively. Selective consideration of inputs is a routine ISO 27001 audit finding that this procedure prevents.

## 4.1  The Seven Mandatory Inputs

| Input | Clause 9.3.2 Specification | BFS Implementation |

| --- | --- | --- |

| 9.3.2(a) | The status of actions from previous management reviews | Section 4.2 — review of all open actions from prior Management Reviews; closure verification; update on partial-completion items; documentation of any actions becoming infeasible or superseded |

| 9.3.2(b) | Changes in external and internal issues that are relevant to the ISMS | Section 4.3 — explicit review of changes to context per BFS-ISMS-002 §2 and §3; external issues (regulatory, threat, market); internal issues (strategy, organization, technology) |

| 9.3.2(c) | Changes in needs and expectations of interested parties | Section 4.4 — review of changes to interested parties register from BFS-ISMS-002 §3; new interested parties; changed expectations; emerging stakeholder concerns |

| 9.3.2(d) | Feedback on information security performance, including trends in: (1) nonconformities and corrective actions; (2) monitoring and measurement results; (3) audit results; (4) fulfilment of information security objectives | Section 4.5 — comprehensive performance review across four dimensions (1) BFS-ISMS-010 corrective action register; (2) operational metrics from BFS-ISP series; (3) BFS-ISMS-008 Internal Audit aggregate findings; (4) BFS-ISMS-005 objectives progress against quantitative targets |

| 9.3.2(e) | Feedback from interested parties | Section 4.6 — feedback collected from regulators (BSP examination outcomes, NPC interactions, certification body), customers (complaints, surveys, breach notification feedback), employees (security culture survey, awareness program feedback), service providers, and Board members |

| 9.3.2(f) | Results of risk assessment and status of risk treatment plan | Section 4.7 — review of BFS-ISMS-003 Risk Register state; material risk changes; emerging risks; treatment plan progress; risk acceptance reviews |

| 9.3.2(g) | Opportunities for continual improvement | Section 4.8 — proactive identification of improvement opportunities — peer institution practices, industry benchmarks, technology evolution, framework updates, lessons from sector incidents |

## 4.2  Input 9.3.2(a) — Status of Actions from Previous Management Reviews

Every Management Review begins with examination of actions assigned at the prior Management Review. This examination ensures that decisions actually translate to action and that incomplete actions are addressed rather than allowed to lapse. Materials prepared:

Action Register Update: For each action assigned at the prior review, the current status — Closed (action completed and verified), In Progress (work underway with target date), At Risk (action behind schedule with mitigation plan), Superseded (event has rendered action irrelevant), Deferred (target date moved with justification);

Closure Evidence: For Closed actions, brief evidence summary supporting closure determination — e.g., 'Action: implement quarterly Privileged Access Reviews. Closed: BFS-ISP-005 §5.5 reviewed and updated to require quarterly reviews; Q3 and Q4 2024 reviews completed and documented.';

Open Action Details: For In Progress and At Risk actions, target completion dates, accountable parties, and progress narrative;

Cumulative Action Trend: Trend analysis of action completion timeliness over time — improving, stable, or deteriorating discipline of execution against Management Review decisions.

Material non-completion of prior Management Review actions is itself a finding for the current review. Repeated non-completion across reviews indicates governance weakness requiring direct Committee attention.

## 4.3  Input 9.3.2(b) — Changes in External and Internal Issues

Per BFS-ISMS-002 (Scope and Context), BFS maintains documented external and internal issues affecting the ISMS. Changes to these issues since the prior Management Review are identified and reviewed. Materials prepared:

### 4.3.1  External Issues

Regulatory landscape changes: New BSP circulars or amendments; NPC issuances; PCI DSS revisions; SEC governance updates; RA 11765 implementing rules; international regulatory developments (e.g., EU AI Act, GDPR enforcement evolution) with potential cross-border relevance;

Threat landscape evolution: Material new threat categories; sector-specific threat intelligence; significant peer institution incidents and lessons; emerging threats to financial services and AI-native platforms; threat actor capability changes;

Technology landscape changes: Material technology evolution affecting ISMS — AI/LLM advances, quantum computing developments, cloud security evolution, identity and authentication evolution;

Market and competitive landscape: Sector consolidation; competitive ISMS posture trends; customer expectations evolution; partner ecosystem changes;

Sector developments: BSP-ISAC and FS-ISAC information sharing; sector-wide initiatives; trade association activities affecting ISMS expectations.

### 4.3.2  Internal Issues

Strategic direction changes: Material BFS strategy updates; new business lines; geographic expansion; product strategy changes;

Organizational changes: Material restructuring; new senior leadership; CISO Office evolution; business unit restructuring affecting ISMS;

Technology architecture changes: AI-native platform development progress; cloud migration progress; legacy system retirement; major technology refresh;

Capability evolution: ISMS function maturity progression; staff growth or contraction; capability building in emerging areas (AI security, cloud security);

Resource changes: ISMS budget evolution; allocation changes; new investment commitments.

## 4.4  Input 9.3.2(c) — Changes in Interested Party Needs and Expectations

BFS-ISMS-002 §3 documents BFS interested parties and their needs and expectations. Changes since prior Management Review are identified. Materials prepared:

New interested parties identified during the period;

Changes in expectations of existing interested parties — e.g., elevated regulatory expectations from BSP cybersecurity maturity assessments, changed customer expectations on transparency after sector incidents, new vendor expectations on supply chain transparency;

Emerging concerns expressed by interested parties through formal channels — regulatory communications, customer feedback, employee surveys, vendor relationships, certification body engagements;

Implications of changes for ISMS — whether scope (BFS-ISMS-002), risk methodology (BFS-ISMS-003), Statement of Applicability (BFS-ISMS-004), objectives (BFS-ISMS-005), or operational policies should be updated.

## 4.5  Input 9.3.2(d) — Feedback on Information Security Performance

Clause 9.3.2(d) explicitly enumerates four sub-inputs covering information security performance trends. Each is addressed comprehensively at every Management Review:

### 4.5.1  Sub-input (1): Trends in Nonconformities and Corrective Actions

Materials drawn from BFS-ISMS-010 (Nonconformity and Corrective Action Register):

Nonconformities identified during the period — count by source (Internal Audit, regulatory examination, certification audit, incident, self-assessment), severity (CRITICAL/MAJOR/MINOR), and domain;

Corrective action register status — open count, closed count, average closure time, repeat findings;

Trend analysis — period-over-period and year-over-year trends in nonconformity volume, severity, and remediation timeliness;

Material themes — patterns in nonconformities suggesting systemic issues (e.g., repeated finding categories, recurring root causes);

Risk-rated outstanding nonconformities — open CRITICAL/MAJOR findings exceeding remediation timelines, requiring Committee attention.

### 4.5.2  Sub-input (2): Monitoring and Measurement Results

Materials drawn from operational ISMS monitoring across the BFS-ISP series:

Operational KPI dashboard — comprehensive view of metrics from BFS-ISP-001 through BFS-ISP-018 §10 (Compliance and Enforcement KPIs sections);

Material variances from targets — KPIs significantly above or below targets with analysis;

Trend analysis — KPI trends over multiple periods identifying improving or deteriorating performance;

Cross-domain integration — relationships between metrics across different BFS-ISP policies suggesting systemic strengths or weaknesses;

Comparison to benchmarks — peer financial institution comparisons where available, BSP sector benchmarks, FS-ISAC trends.

### 4.5.3  Sub-input (3): Audit Results

Materials drawn from BFS-ISMS-008 (Internal Audit Programme):

Audit Programme execution status — audits completed against plan; coverage of high-risk areas; programme adjustments and rationale;

Aggregate findings analysis — total findings by severity, domain, and audit area; comparison to prior period;

Audit opinions issued — distribution across Effective / Substantially Effective / Needs Improvement / Ineffective;

Remediation status — audit findings remediated within target timelines (target ≥90% per BFS-ISMS-005 O8.1);

External audit results — ISO 27001 certification body audits, PCI QSA assessments, BSP examinations, NPC reviews;

Internal Audit function quality — external quality assessment results when available, programme self-assessment outcomes.

### 4.5.4  Sub-input (4): Fulfilment of Information Security Objectives

Materials drawn from BFS-ISMS-005 (Information Security Objectives):

Quarterly progress against all 41 operational objectives — RAG status, performance against quantitative targets;

Strategic objective progress — performance across the eight strategic objectives O1-O8 from BFS-ISMS-001 §3.2;

Material variances — objectives at AMBER or RED status with corrective action plans;

Year-end performance for the Annual Management Review — final achievement assessment for the calendar year, recognized achievements, and lessons learned;

Forthcoming-year objectives proposal — for the Annual Management Review, the proposed CY+1 operational objectives developed by CISO Office for Committee approval.

## 4.6  Input 9.3.2(e) — Feedback from Interested Parties

Beyond changes to expectations addressed in Section 4.4, this input addresses operational feedback received during the period. Materials prepared:

| Interested Party | Feedback Channels | Feedback Categories Addressed |

| --- | --- | --- |

| Regulators (BSP, NPC, SEC, AMLC) | Examination outcomes; correspondence; consultation submissions; supervisory letters | Regulatory expectations evolution; specific findings; emerging supervisory focus areas |

| Certification Body (ISO 27001) | Audit findings (initial and surveillance); pre-assessment feedback | Certification readiness gaps; strengths recognized; emerging Standard interpretations |

| PCI QSA | Annual assessment outcomes; interim observations | PCI DSS compliance posture; specific findings; emerging requirements |

| Customers | Complaints register; customer satisfaction surveys; breach notification feedback; transaction support feedback | Customer expectations on security and privacy; specific complaint themes; trust survey scores |

| Employees | Security culture survey; awareness program feedback; security event reporting analysis; whistleblower channel reports | Internal cultural perceptions; awareness program effectiveness; concerns about ISMS practices |

| Service Providers | Vendor risk assessments; contract review discussions; supplier-reported incidents | Bilateral concerns; supply chain insights; cooperation effectiveness |

| Board Members | Board observations during quarterly reporting; written Board questions; Committee deliberations | Board strategic concerns; emerging risk perceptions; oversight expectations |

| Sector ISACs | BSP-ISAC and FS-ISAC engagement | Sector-wide patterns; lessons from peer incidents; collective threat intelligence |

## 4.7  Input 9.3.2(f) — Risk Assessment Results and Risk Treatment Status

Materials drawn from BFS-ISMS-003 (Risk Assessment and Treatment Methodology) and the BFS Risk Register:

Risk Register state — total risks tracked, distribution across Risk Tier (CRITICAL/HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW), trends since prior Management Review;

Material new risks — risks identified during the period with material residual risk requiring Committee awareness;

Material risk changes — risks where assessed likelihood or consequence has materially changed, including risks elevated due to threat landscape evolution or risks reduced through control implementation;

Risk treatment plan progress — progress on treatment actions for CRITICAL and HIGH risks; treatment effectiveness assessment; materially delayed treatments;

Risk acceptance reviews — Committee-level review of accepted risks (per BFS-ISMS-003 §6.5) including periodic re-validation that acceptance remains appropriate;

Annual methodology calibration — for the Annual Management Review, presentation of the BFS-ISMS-003 §9.4 methodology calibration review with proposed improvements;

Integration with BFS-ISMS-004 (SoA) — material changes to SoA implementation status driven by risk-driven control changes.

## 4.8  Input 9.3.2(g) — Opportunities for Continual Improvement

Per ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 10.1, BFS shall continually improve the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the ISMS. Improvement opportunities are proactively identified — they are not solely reactions to nonconformities. Materials prepared:

Peer benchmarking — comparison to peer financial institutions in Philippine market and globally on ISMS practices, where information is available;

Industry framework evolution — relevant updates to ISO 27001/27002, NIST CSF, CIS Controls, and other frameworks that BFS could selectively adopt;

Technology opportunity analysis — emerging technologies that could enhance ISMS effectiveness (AI/ML for detection, deception technology, behavioral analytics, automated remediation);

Process maturity advancement — opportunities to increase maturity of specific ISMS processes from current level to next level using maturity framework benchmarks;

Lessons from peer incidents — incidents at peer institutions or in adjacent sectors providing learning opportunities — what defenses prevented worse outcomes, what control gaps were exposed;

Internal observations — opportunities surfaced by ISMS personnel, operational managers, or external assessors not yet rising to formal nonconformity but warranting management consideration;

Strategic alignment opportunities — opportunities to better align ISMS with BFS strategic direction (AI-native platform, customer experience evolution, regulatory positioning).

# 5.  MANAGEMENT REVIEW PROCESS

This section establishes the standardized process for conducting individual Management Reviews — preparation, conduct, output documentation, and follow-up. The process is applied uniformly across bi-annual scheduled reviews and out-of-cycle reviews, calibrated for scope appropriately.

## 5.1  Process Phases

| Phase | Phase Name | Typical Duration | Key Outputs |

| --- | --- | --- | --- |

| Phase 1 | Preparation | 6-8 weeks before Forum | Input package compiled; agenda finalized; participants notified; pre-read materials distributed |

| Phase 2 | Pre-Review Briefing | 1-2 weeks before Forum | CISO briefing to Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair; agenda confirmation; question previews |

| Phase 3 | Forum Conduct | Forum date | Management Review session conducted; deliberation; decisions made; actions assigned |

| Phase 4 | Documentation | Within 14 days after Forum | Draft Minutes prepared; reviewed; finalized; formally signed off; distributed |

| Phase 5 | Action Tracking | Continuous until next Forum | Action register maintained; progress tracked; status reported at next Management Review |

## 5.2  Phase 1: Preparation

Preparation begins 6-8 weeks before the scheduled Management Review Forum. The CISO Office leads preparation with input contributions from the wider ISMS function and external assurance providers. Preparation phase activities:

### 5.2.1  Input Package Compilation

Input source coordination: CISO Office issues data calls to BFS-ISMS-008 (Internal Audit), BFS-ISMS-010 (Corrective Action), BFS-ISMS-003 (Risk Register custodian), BFS-ISP policy owners, HR (training and culture data), Compliance (regulatory feedback), DPO (privacy posture), CIO (technology context), and other input sources;

Data validation: Inputs received from sources are validated for currency, completeness, and accuracy. Material data quality issues are escalated for resolution before package finalization;

Synthesis and analysis: CISO Office synthesizes inputs into the Management Review Input Package — translating raw data into Committee-appropriate analysis. Trends are identified, themes are surfaced, recommendations are framed;

Format standardization: Input package follows standard format ensuring all seven Clause 9.3.2 inputs are explicitly addressed in dedicated sections — even when there is little to report on a particular input, the section confirms 'no material change since prior review' rather than omitting the section;

Quality review: Input package reviewed by CISO and Internal Audit before submission to Committee. Internal Audit independent review provides assurance that input package fairly represents ISMS state — not selectively favorable;

Length calibration: Input package targets readability — typical packages are 30-60 pages with executive summary, detailed sections per Clause 9.3.2 input, and supporting appendices. Inflated packages disrespect Committee time; under-detailed packages prevent informed deliberation.

### 5.2.2  Standard Input Package Structure

The Management Review Input Package follows a standardized structure ensuring auditor-friendly traceability and consistent Committee experience:

| Section | Title | Content |

| --- | --- | --- |

| 1 | Executive Summary | Single-page overview — key themes, headline metrics, principal recommendations, decisions sought |

| 2 | Status of Prior Actions [Input 9.3.2(a)] | Action register update from prior reviews; closure evidence; open actions |

| 3 | Context Changes [Input 9.3.2(b)] | External and internal issues changes; implications for ISMS |

| 4 | Interested Parties Updates [Input 9.3.2(c)] | Changes to stakeholders, expectations, emerging concerns |

| 5 | Performance Feedback [Input 9.3.2(d)] | Four sub-sections: nonconformities and CA; monitoring/measurement; audit results; objectives fulfilment |

| 6 | Interested Parties Feedback [Input 9.3.2(e)] | Operational feedback during the period from regulators, customers, employees, etc. |

| 7 | Risk Assessment and Treatment [Input 9.3.2(f)] | Risk Register state; material changes; treatment plan progress; risk acceptance reviews |

| 8 | Improvement Opportunities [Input 9.3.2(g)] | Proactively identified opportunities; benchmarking; framework evolution |

| 9 | Decisions Sought | Specific decisions requested of the Committee — improvements, changes, resource allocation, objectives approval |

| 10 | Appendices | Supporting evidence; detailed metrics; specific reports referenced in main sections |

### 5.2.3  Distribution and Pre-Read

Final input package distributed to all Forum participants at minimum 14 days before Forum date — adequate time for participants to read, consider, and prepare questions;

Distribution is electronic with appropriate access controls per CONFIDENTIAL classification;

Hard-copy alternatives available upon request from Committee members preferring paper review;

Companion materials (full audit reports, full corrective action register, full Risk Register) available upon request for participants seeking depth beyond the input package summary;

Distribution confirmation tracked — CISO Office verifies receipt by Committee members, escalating non-receipt promptly.

## 5.3  Phase 2: Pre-Review Briefing

In the 1-2 weeks before the Forum, the CISO conducts a pre-review briefing with the Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair to ensure Forum effectiveness:

Agenda walkthrough — confirming time allocation across the Clause 9.3.2 inputs and decisions sought;

Material themes preview — orienting the Chair to the principal themes the Forum will address;

Anticipated discussion points — identifying topics likely to require extended deliberation or where divergent views may emerge;

Question previews — sharing anticipated Committee questions with the CISO Office so prepared responses can be readied;

External attendees confirmation — confirming any external participants (certification body, advisors) and their expected contributions;

Logistical confirmation — Forum venue, duration, support arrangements.

## 5.4  Phase 3: Forum Conduct

The Management Review Forum is conducted at scheduled Board Audit & Risk Committee meetings with extended agenda dedicated to ISMS Management Review. Standard Forum protocol:

### 5.4.1  Opening

Quorum confirmation: Chair confirms quorum present (per Section 3.4) before substantive deliberation;

Agenda confirmation: Forum agenda confirmed; any agenda adjustments noted;

Prior actions status: First substantive item — review of actions from prior Management Review per Input 9.3.2(a). This protocol convention reinforces accountability for prior decisions;

### 5.4.2  Input Review

Each of the seven Clause 9.3.2 inputs is addressed in dedicated agenda time:

Input presentation: CISO presents the input section briefly — anchoring on the input package material rather than rehearsing it. Committee members are expected to have read pre-read materials;

Committee deliberation: Committee members raise questions, observations, and concerns. CISO and other Standing Attendees respond. Discussion is open and substantive;

Implications synthesis: Discussion converges on implications — what the input means for ISMS suitability, adequacy, or effectiveness;

Decision preparation: Where input deliberation suggests decisions are needed, preliminary decision framing is captured for the Decisions phase.

### 5.4.3  Decisions

After all seven inputs have been reviewed, the Forum addresses decisions per Clause 9.3.3 (detailed in Section 6 of this procedure). Decisions cover:

Continual improvement opportunities — which improvements to pursue, with assigned accountability and target timelines;

Changes to the ISMS — material changes approved (scope, methodology, controls, objectives, resources);

Specific actions — assigned to named accountable parties with target dates;

Reaffirmations — explicit reaffirmation that the ISMS continues to be suitable, adequate, and effective (or qualified affirmation noting specific concerns).

### 5.4.4  Closing

Action summary: Chair summarizes actions assigned, naming accountable parties and target dates;

Out-of-cycle review trigger discussion: Committee considers whether any input or decision rises to a level requiring out-of-cycle review attention before next scheduled Forum;

Next steps: Confirmation of Minutes development timeline and next Forum scheduling;

Forum closure: Chair declares Forum closed.

## 5.5  Phase 4: Documentation

Documentation of Management Review is the documented information required by Clause 9.3.3. Without proper documentation, the Forum's deliberation is unavailable for ISO 27001 audit verification, regulatory examination, or operational follow-up.

### 5.5.1  Minutes Content

Management Review Minutes shall include the following content:

Forum metadata: Date, time, location (or virtual), duration; whether scheduled bi-annual or out-of-cycle;

Quorum confirmation: Decision-Making Participants present; Standing and Specific Attendees present;

Materials reviewed: Reference to Management Review Input Package version distributed (e.g., 'Management Review Input Package v1.0 dated [date]');

Input deliberation summary: For each of the seven Clause 9.3.2 inputs, summary of deliberation — key points raised, concerns expressed, clarifications obtained, implications synthesized. Need not be verbatim transcript but shall be substantive enough that an auditor can verify each input was addressed;

Decisions made: For each decision per Clause 9.3.3 — explicit decision statement, supporting rationale, dissenting views if any (with attribution where appropriate);

Actions assigned: For each action — description, accountable party (named role), target date, success criteria, tracking mechanism;

Suitability/Adequacy/Effectiveness opinion: Explicit Committee opinion on whether the ISMS continues to be suitable, adequate, and effective — qualified or unqualified;

Next Forum: Date for next Management Review (next scheduled bi-annual unless out-of-cycle event intervenes);

Sign-off: Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair signature confirming Minutes accurately reflect Forum deliberation and decisions.

### 5.5.2  Minutes Workflow

Draft Minutes prepared by Committee Secretary (with CISO Office support) within 7 days of Forum;

Draft circulated to Forum participants for review and correction within 5 business days;

Material disagreements about Minutes content are resolved through the Chair — typically by adjustment to capture deliberation more accurately rather than altering documented decisions;

Final Minutes signed by Chair within 14 days of Forum;

Final Minutes distributed to: Board Audit & Risk Committee, full Board (for awareness), CEO, Decision-Making Participants, Standing Attendees, Internal Audit, and added to BFS Document Management System per BFS-ISMS-006;

Minutes are CONFIDENTIAL classification (per BFS-ISMS-006 §3.4) with retention of at least 7 years per BFS-ISMS-006 §6.3.

## 5.6  Phase 5: Action Tracking

Decisions and actions assigned at Management Review are tracked through to closure:

Action registration: All actions registered in the BFS Management Review Action Register maintained by CISO Office. Register includes action description, accountable party, target date, status, and reference to originating Management Review;

Progress tracking: Quarterly progress updates collected from accountable parties; status reported to Information Security Steering Committee monthly and to Board Audit & Risk Committee quarterly;

At-risk action escalation: Actions falling behind target dates are escalated to CISO and accountable executives for intervention; persistent at-risk actions are escalated to Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair;

Closure verification: Action closure requires evidence that the action was completed — not just claimed completion. Verification is performed by CISO Office (operational closure) and validated by Internal Audit periodically (assurance closure);

Reporting at next Management Review: Action register status is the first agenda item at every subsequent Management Review per Input 9.3.2(a) — closing the loop between decisions and accountability.

# 6.  MANAGEMENT REVIEW OUTPUTS AND DECISIONS

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3.3 specifies that the outputs of management review shall include decisions related to: (a) continual improvement opportunities; and (b) any need for changes to the ISMS. Documented information shall be retained as evidence of the results of management reviews. This section operationalizes these requirements at BFS.

## 6.1  Output Categories

BFS Management Review produces outputs in four categories — addressing the Clause 9.3.3 requirements while providing operational structure:

| Output Category | Description | Examples |

| --- | --- | --- |

| Suitability/Adequacy/Effectiveness Opinion | Explicit Committee opinion on whether the ISMS continues to meet Clause 9.3.1 expectations | Unqualified affirmation; qualified affirmation noting specific concerns; identification of material concerns requiring out-of-cycle attention |

| Continual Improvement Decisions | Decisions to pursue specific improvement opportunities | Adopt new framework element; pilot new technology; expand existing program scope; benchmark against peer practice |

| ISMS Change Decisions | Decisions to materially change ISMS | Scope change (BFS-ISMS-002); methodology change (BFS-ISMS-003); SoA changes (BFS-ISMS-004); objective changes (BFS-ISMS-005); policy changes |

| Resource and Direction Decisions | Decisions on ISMS resources and strategic direction | Budget allocation adjustments; staffing additions; investment priorities; strategic priority changes |

## 6.2  Suitability, Adequacy, and Effectiveness Opinion

Per Clause 9.3.1, Management Review must determine whether the ISMS continues to be suitable, adequate, and effective. This opinion is the synthesis output of the Forum — distinct from operational performance metrics that feed into it. The opinion is rendered explicitly at every Management Review, recorded in Minutes, and is itself a controlled output.

| Opinion Category | Definition | Implications |

| --- | --- | --- |

| Unqualified Affirmation | ISMS continues to be suitable, adequate, and effective without material concerns | Routine continuation; no special action; periodic review continues |

| Qualified Affirmation | ISMS continues to be suitable, adequate, and effective overall but with specific concerns warranting attention | Specific concerns documented; corrective action assigned; status reported at next Management Review or sooner if material |

| Material Concerns Identified | ISMS has material concerns regarding suitability, adequacy, or effectiveness requiring focused intervention | Out-of-cycle Management Review may be triggered within 6 months; intensive corrective action; potentially escalated to full Board |

| Fundamental Concerns | Rare — fundamental questions about ISMS validity (e.g., post-major-incident, post-acquisition that materially changes scope) | Comprehensive ISMS reassessment; potentially full re-baseline of risk assessment, SoA, and objectives; full Board engagement |

Most Management Reviews will produce Unqualified or Qualified Affirmations — reflecting that the ISMS is operating substantially as designed even in the presence of specific issues. Material Concerns and Fundamental Concerns opinions are rare events. The opinion is documented in Minutes with supporting rationale referencing specific inputs and findings.

## 6.3  Continual Improvement Decisions

Per Clause 9.3.3(a), Management Review identifies and decides on continual improvement opportunities. Improvement decisions are deliberate enhancements beyond correction of nonconformity. Sources of improvement opportunities (per Section 4.8) include peer benchmarking, framework evolution, technology opportunities, process maturity advancement, lessons from peer incidents, internal observations, and strategic alignment opportunities.

Decision dimensions for each improvement opportunity:

Adoption: Yes (proceed) / No (decline with rationale) / Defer (adopt later with target date) / Modify (adopt modified version);

Resource allocation: Internal staff time; budget allocation; external advisor engagement;

Implementation approach: Phased pilot; full deployment; trial implementation with go/no-go review;

Timeline: Target completion date with milestone checkpoints;

Success criteria: How achievement of the improvement will be measured;

Accountability: Named role responsible for implementation;

Reporting: Status reporting cadence to Information Security Steering Committee and Board Audit & Risk Committee.

## 6.4  ISMS Change Decisions

Per Clause 9.3.3(b), Management Review identifies and decides on changes needed in the ISMS. ISMS changes are material modifications affecting fundamental ISMS elements:

| Change Category | Affected Documents | Implementation Pathway |

| --- | --- | --- |

| ISMS Scope Change | BFS-ISMS-002 | Scope revision; SoA re-evaluation; certification body notification if certified |

| Risk Methodology Change | BFS-ISMS-003 | Methodology revision; potentially Risk Register re-baseline |

| Statement of Applicability Change | BFS-ISMS-004 | Applicability re-determination; implementation pathway update |

| Objectives Change | BFS-ISMS-005 | Objective revision (typically annual reset); targets re-baseline; reporting cadence reaffirmation |

| Top-Level Policy Change | BFS-ISMS-001 | Policy revision; full Board approval required for material changes |

| Subordinate Policy Change | BFS-ISP series | Policy revision per BFS-ISMS-006; co-approver re-approval for material changes |

| Governance Structure Change | BFS-ISMS-001 §5; possibly other documents | Governance change; communication plan; transition management |

| Resource Allocation Change | Budget and resource records | Budget revision; HR planning; vendor arrangements |

Material ISMS changes typically have implementation pathways extending beyond the Management Review Forum — change is decided in principle at Forum and detailed implementation proceeds afterward. Implementation progress is reported back at subsequent Management Reviews.

## 6.5  Documented Information Per Clause 9.3.3

Per Clause 9.3.3, BFS shall retain documented information as evidence of the results of management reviews. This documentation requirement is the principal artifact ISO 27001 certification body auditors examine for Clause 9.3 compliance:

Management Review Minutes — primary evidence of Forum conduct and decisions; retained per BFS-ISMS-006 (typically 7 years for ISMS records);

Management Review Input Packages — retained as documentation of inputs considered;

Action Register — current state with closure evidence; retained indefinitely while actions remain open and archived for 7 years after closure;

Supporting evidence — referenced documents, audit reports, risk register snapshots, and other materials that informed Forum deliberation;

Sign-off records — Chair signatures and Forum participant attestations of Minutes accuracy.

Documentation completeness is verified through Internal Audit per BFS-ISMS-008. Material documentation gaps are themselves nonconformities for tracking through BFS-ISMS-010.

## 6.6  Integration with the Operational ISMS

Management Review outputs are not isolated — they propagate through the ISMS via documented integration:

BFS-ISMS-010 (Corrective Action): Management Review actions and decisions requiring operational execution are registered in the Corrective Action Register, where remediation is managed, tracked, and verified using the same operational discipline applied to audit findings;

BFS-ISMS-005 (Information Security Objectives): Decisions affecting objectives — new objectives, target adjustments, decommissioning of objectives — are operationalized through BFS-ISMS-005 update process;

BFS-ISMS-003 (Risk Methodology) and BFS-ISMS-004 (SoA): Decisions affecting risk methodology or control applicability are operationalized through corresponding document revision per BFS-ISMS-006;

BFS-ISMS-008 (Internal Audit): Management Review may direct adjustments to the Audit Programme — additional audit attention to specific areas, deferred audits, or new audit scope reflecting strategic priorities;

BFS-ISMS-007 (Communication Plan): Management Review decisions affecting external communications (regulatory submissions, customer communications, sector engagement) are operationalized through the communication framework;

Resource allocation: Decisions affecting ISMS resources are operationalized through annual budget process and ad-hoc resource adjustments — with appropriate Board financial oversight.

This integration ensures that Management Review is not theatre — the documented decisions actually drive ISMS evolution. Failure of integration (decisions documented but not operationalized) is itself a nonconformity tracked through BFS-ISMS-010.

## 8.2  Internal Audit Verification

Internal Audit (per BFS-ISMS-008) verifies Management Review effectiveness through:

Annual audit of the Management Review process (Domain A, Audit Universe per BFS-ISMS-008 §3.2);

Verification that all Clause 9.3.2 inputs are systematically addressed by examining recent Management Review Minutes;

Verification that decisions are documented with appropriate detail per Section 5.5;

Verification that actions are tracked to closure with closure evidence;

Sample testing of action closure evidence — confirming closures are substantive rather than nominal;

Identification of Management Review process nonconformities for tracking through BFS-ISMS-010 Corrective Action.

## 8.3  Effectiveness Indicators Beyond Compliance

Compliance metrics (e.g., 100% inputs addressed, 100% Minutes signed) are necessary but not sufficient indicators of Management Review effectiveness. Effectiveness is measured through outcome indicators:

Material decisions made — the proportion of Management Reviews producing material decisions vs. routine confirmations;

Quality of deliberation — depth of Committee engagement evident in Minutes; substantive challenges and clarifications;

Action implementation rate — whether decisions translate into operational ISMS evolution;

Improvement opportunity uptake — proportion of identified opportunities pursued vs. declined;

Strategic ISMS direction setting — evidence that Management Review actually steers ISMS direction rather than ratifying decisions made elsewhere;

Forum participant engagement — pre-read evidence (questions submitted in advance), substantive deliberation rather than presentation-only sessions;

Cross-Management-Review coherence — consistency of strategic direction across successive reviews; coherent evolution rather than oscillation.

| What outputs must the Management Review produce? | Per Clause 9.3.3: (1) explicit Suitability/Adequacy/Effectiveness opinion; (2) continual improvement decisions; (3) ISMS change decisions; (4) actions assigned with accountability and target dates. Documented in Minutes. See Section 6. |

| Who signs the Management Review Minutes? | Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair signs Minutes within 14 days of Forum. See Section 5.5.2. |

| What happens to Management Review actions after the Forum? | Registered in Management Review Action Register (CISO Office); progress tracked quarterly; reported at every subsequent Management Review per Input 9.3.2(a); closed only with closure evidence. See Section 5.6. |

| What if a material event occurs between scheduled reviews? | Out-of-cycle Management Review triggered per Section 3.5 — CRITICAL incidents with material impact; material adverse audit findings; scope changes; material regulatory changes; Board direction; fundamental ISMS concerns. Same procedure as scheduled reviews. |

| How long are Management Review records retained? | Minimum 7 years for ISMS records per BFS-ISMS-006 §6.3. Action Register entries persist beyond closure for traceability. See Section 6.5. |

| Where does this fit in the overall ISMS governance? | Strategic counterpart to operational Internal Audit (BFS-ISMS-008). Receives inputs from BFS-ISMS-003/004/005/008/010 and other ISMS sources. Outputs propagate to BFS-ISMS-005 (objectives), BFS-ISMS-010 (corrective action), BFS-ISMS-008 (audit programme adjustments), and underlying policies. See Section 6.6. |

| What if I think the Management Review is not effective? | Raise concerns through Internal Audit (BFS-ISMS-008) which audits the Management Review process annually. Material concerns can be escalated to Board Audit & Risk Committee Chair directly or through whistleblower channel per BFS-ISMS-001 §11.3. Non-retaliation protected. |
